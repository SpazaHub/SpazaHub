About

A spaza shop, also known as a tuck shop, is an informal convenience shop business in South Africa. It's usually set up at someone's home. These shops sell basic things people need every day. They also help the owners make extra money for their families. There is often a number of them within an area and due to their limited stock, shoppers often find themselves needing to visit multiple spazas to find all the items they need on a shopping run. In addition to this, spazas often close and reopen at irregular hours since the owners run the shops themselves and sometimes have other matters to attend to. The often manual manner of record keeping also makes it difficult to identify stock losses, budget, and pre-empt stock shortages. This project aims to equip the spaza shop owners with the ability to manage their stock efficiently. The platform will also equip buyers with the ability to order their cart from multiple shops and get a view of an efficient journey to collect their goods once the order has been placed.
